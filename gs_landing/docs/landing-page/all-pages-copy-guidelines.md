# GardenSuite All Pages - Copy Guidelines

## Purpose

This is the master copy guide for every GardenSuite website page and component.

It applies to:

- homepage
- product pages
- navigation
- footer
- CTAs
- FAQ
- trust sections
- proof sections
- SEO titles and meta descriptions
- future pages
- emails or short contact text if written in the same voice

The standard:

`Clear first. Trustworthy second. Polished third.`

GardenSuite can look premium, but the copy must never become hard to understand.

## Golden Rule

Every line must pass this test:

`Will a busy tea garden owner, manager, or office staff member understand this in one reading?`

If the answer is no, rewrite it.

## Audience Reality

Write for people who:

- run or manage tea gardens
- handle attendance, payroll, factory, stores, and reports
- may not be fully fluent in English
- may be older or more comfortable with direct business language
- may distrust public claims without proof
- care more about on-site support than self-service software
- understand tea garden work better than software words

Do not write first for:

- startup investors
- generic SaaS buyers
- software developers
- corporate procurement teams only
- people who need trendy product language

## Product Positioning

Internal positioning:

`GardenSuite is an offline-first tea garden management system by Sarbani Associates. It connects face attendance, smart weighing, payroll, factory production, stores, and daily reports.`

Plain page-level version:

`GardenSuite helps tea gardens manage attendance, leaf weight, payroll, factory, stores, and daily reports from one system. It works offline at the garden and syncs when internet is available.`

Short version:

`Tea garden software for attendance, weighing, payroll, factory, stores, and daily reports.`

## Brand Voice

GardenSuite should sound:

- simple
- specific
- practical
- calm
- local
- experienced
- trustworthy
- respectful
- grounded in tea garden work

GardenSuite should not sound:

- inflated
- dramatic
- startup-generic
- overly technical
- foreign to tea garden operations
- clever at the cost of clarity
- like a self-service SaaS tool with no local support

## Language Level

Write for simple English.

Rules:

- one idea per sentence
- short paragraphs
- short section headings
- common words before technical words
- real workflow words before benefits
- avoid long clauses
- avoid idioms that non-fluent readers may miss

Good:

`Attendance works offline at the garden. Data syncs when internet returns.`

Bad:

`Our resilient offline-first architecture ensures uninterrupted workforce continuity.`

## Mandatory Writing Rules

- Do not use em dashes.
- Use regular hyphens only.
- Keep sentences short.
- Prefer active voice.
- Use `you`, `your garden`, and `your staff` where useful.
- Mention Sarbani Associates in footer and trust-building sections.
- Tie every claim to a mechanism.
- Use real tea garden vocabulary.
- Respect client confidentiality.
- Do not invent numbers, client names, results, or guarantees.

## Core Message Order

A visitor should understand these points in this order:

1. GardenSuite is for tea gardens.
2. It handles attendance, leaf weight, payroll, factory, stores, and daily reports.
3. It works offline at the garden.
4. It connects field data to office work.
5. Owners and managers can check daily numbers from anywhere.
6. Sarbani Associates installs, trains, and supports on site.
7. Many client names are private, but region-level trust can be shown.
8. The next step is a free demo.

If a page skips the first 4 points, it is not clear enough.

## Page-Type Guidelines

### Homepage

Job:

- define GardenSuite quickly
- create trust
- show the system scope
- route visitors to product pages or demo

Must include:

- simple hero
- connected module list
- offline claim
- Sarbani Associates trust cue
- anonymous or region-level proof
- clear CTA

Good hero direction:

`Run your tea garden. Not your paperwork.`

Good support line:

`Offline software for face attendance, smart weighing, payroll, factory, stores, and daily reporting.`

Avoid:

- making the hero only poetic
- hiding what the product does
- making the site feel like generic business software

### Product Pages

Job:

- explain one module
- show the old problem
- show the GardenSuite workflow
- reduce setup fear
- give a clear demo step

Use this structure:

1. Hero outcome
2. Old way problem
3. Workflow steps
4. Result or metric strip
5. On-site rollout section
6. Role-specific buyer benefit
7. Confidentiality note
8. CTA

For deeper module-specific rules, use `product-page-copy-guidelines.md`.

### Navigation

Job:

- help users find products quickly
- stay short
- avoid clever labels

Use:

- `Products`
- `Features`
- `About`
- `Contact`
- `Book Free Demo`

Product labels:

- `Face Attendance & Smart Weighing`
- `Automated Payroll`
- `Factory Production`
- `Store Management`
- `Daily Report`

Avoid:

- `Platform`
- `Solutions`
- `Insights`
- `Experience`
- `Resources` unless those pages exist and matter

### Footer

Job:

- confirm brand, company, support, and contact
- make Sarbani Associates visible

Must include:

- GardenSuite name
- Sarbani Associates
- Bagdogra, Siliguri
- contact email or phone if available
- product links
- simple support/demo link

Good:

`Complete tea garden management software. Built and supported by Sarbani Associates, Bagdogra, Siliguri.`

Avoid:

- generic company blurbs
- hiding parent company name
- legal-looking copy that feels cold

### CTA Sections

Job:

- ask for the next step plainly
- reduce risk
- set expectation

Use:

- `Book Free Demo`
- `Email Us`
- `See GardenSuite with your own garden data`
- `See this module at your garden`

Support copy:

`Demo, on-site setup, and training are free.`

Expectation copy:

`Reply within 1 working day. Demo scheduling call by the Sarbani team.`

Avoid:

- `Start transforming today`
- `Unlock growth now`
- `Get instant access`
- `Request enterprise consultation`

### FAQ

Job:

- answer objections directly
- use very simple language

Good FAQ topics:

- internet/offline use
- setup time
- regions covered
- report access from outside garden
- client name confidentiality
- pricing
- staff training

Good style:

`Yes. Attendance, weighing, payroll, stores, and factory work offline at the garden. Data syncs when internet returns.`

Avoid:

- long answers
- sales language inside answers
- technical explanations unless required

### Trust Sections

Job:

- make the buyer believe GardenSuite is real and supported

Use:

- `20+ tea estates`
- `7 tea-growing regions`
- `Serving tea gardens since 2000`
- `Built and supported by Sarbani Associates`
- `On-site setup and training`
- `Bagdogra, Siliguri`

Confidentiality line:

`Many estates keep software details private. We respect confidentiality and share region-level experience.`

Avoid:

- fake logos
- invented testimonials
- numbers without proof
- hiding why client names are not public

### SEO Titles And Meta Descriptions

Job:

- say what the page is
- include tea garden terms
- stay readable in search

Title pattern:

`[Page Topic] - GardenSuite Tea Garden Software`

Meta pattern:

`[Plain description of module]. Built for tea gardens. Works offline at the garden and is supported by Sarbani Associates.`

Good:

`Face attendance and smart weighing software for tea gardens. Verify workers, record leaf weight, and sync data when internet returns.`

Avoid:

`Advanced biometric ERP solution for seamless workforce optimization.`

## Technical Term Rules

Some technical terms are allowed, but pair them with plain meaning.

Use:

- `Daily Report (MIS Dashboard)`
- `ERP (office software)`
- `online dashboard`
- `syncs when internet returns`
- `PF and ESI reports`

Avoid alone:

- `MIS`
- `ERP`
- `cloud`
- `real-time`
- `statutory compliance`
- `reconciliation`

Good:

`The daily report (MIS dashboard) opens on phone, tablet, or laptop.`

Bad:

`Cloud MIS enables real-time visibility.`

## Claim Rules

Claims must be true, specific, and defensible in a demo.

Use softer operational claims:

- `helps stop proxy attendance`
- `reduces repeated entry`
- `keeps a clear record`
- `works offline`
- `syncs when internet returns`
- `built for tea garden payroll`
- `shows daily numbers from anywhere`

Avoid absolute claims:

- `100% automated`
- `Zero paper`
- `No errors`
- `Stops proxy attendance completely`
- `Never lose track`
- `Guaranteed`

If an absolute claim is used, it must be backed by product behavior and approved by the business.

## Proof Rules

Allowed proof:

- years in business
- regions covered
- module scope
- on-site setup process
- anonymized estate examples
- demo with sample or own garden data
- Sarbani Associates history

Do not use:

- public client names without permission
- exact worker counts unless verified
- made-up ROI
- made-up accuracy numbers
- fake testimonials

## Sarbani Associates Rules

Always position Sarbani Associates as the trust anchor.

Use in:

- footer
- trust strip
- rollout section
- setup/training copy
- about section
- final CTA support where relevant

Good:

`Sarbani Associates installs GardenSuite at your garden, trains staff, and supports rollout.`

Avoid:

`GardenSuite is a self-serve platform you can activate instantly.`

That does not match the sales reality.

## Confidentiality Rules

Client confidentiality is not a weakness. Use it as a trust signal.

Standard copy:

`Many estates keep software details private. We respect confidentiality and share region-level experience.`

Longer version:

`Many tea estates prefer not to disclose their internal software systems. We respect client confidentiality and share region-level experience during demos.`

Use this when:

- a proof section has no logos
- a prospect may ask for client names
- a page needs more trust without public references

## Regional Proof Rules

Use real region names:

- Assam
- Dooars
- Terai
- Darjeeling
- Coochbehar
- Uttar Dinajpur
- Jalpaiguri

Good:

`Used across tea-growing regions including Assam, Dooars, Terai, Darjeeling, Coochbehar, Uttar Dinajpur, and Jalpaiguri.`

Avoid:

`Trusted across India`

Unless the product is actually sold broadly across India.

## Section Heading Rules

Headings should be short and clear.

Good:

- `The problem`
- `The solution`
- `Why GardenSuite`
- `Common questions`
- `How it works`
- `From first call to running live`
- `Trusted across tea-growing regions`

Avoid:

- `Operational intelligence reimagined`
- `The future of estate productivity`
- `A connected ecosystem for tomorrow`

## Buttons And Link Text

Buttons should be direct.

Use:

- `Book Free Demo`
- `Email Us`
- `Learn more`
- `See How It Works`

Avoid:

- `Submit`
- `Get started now`
- `Unlock access`
- `Transform today`

## Page Audit Checklist

Before publishing any page, check:

- Does the first screen say what GardenSuite is?
- Does the page say who it is for?
- Does it name tea garden workflows?
- Does it explain technical terms?
- Does it avoid unsupported absolutes?
- Does it mention Sarbani Associates where trust is needed?
- Does it respect client confidentiality?
- Does it include a clear next step?
- Would a non-fluent English reader understand it?
- Could this copy appear on a generic ERP site? If yes, rewrite it.

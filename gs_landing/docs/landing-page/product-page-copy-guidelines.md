# GardenSuite Product Pages - Copy Audit And Golden Guidelines

## Purpose

This document is the copy standard for GardenSuite product pages:

- Face Attendance & Smart Weighing
- Automated Payroll
- Factory Accounts
- Store Management
- Daily Report (MIS Dashboard)

The goal is simple:

`A tea garden owner, manager, or office staff member should understand the page quickly, trust the claim, and know what to do next.`

Many visitors may not be highly fluent in English. The copy must be clear before it is clever.

## Overall Copy Verdict

The current product pages are directionally strong.

They now do several important things well:

- Each page focuses on one real tea garden problem.
- The pages use practical terms like attendance, leaf weight, hazira, PF, ESI, factory, stores, and daily report.
- Each page shows a simple flow from problem to product to result.
- The rollout section builds confidence that Sarbani Associates installs and trains on site.
- The confidentiality note handles the lack of public client names honestly.
- The CTA area now speaks to specific buyer roles like manager, owner, and office staff.

The remaining risk is language discipline.

Some lines still use broad or slightly technical phrases:

- `real-time`
- `cloud MIS`
- `ERP`
- `100%`
- `Zero`
- `seamlessly`
- `statutory compliance`
- `reconciliation`

These are not banned, but they must be handled carefully. Use them only when the surrounding sentence explains the meaning in plain words.

## Golden Rule

Every line must pass this test:

`Will a busy tea garden buyer understand this in one reading?`

If not, rewrite it.

## Audience

Primary readers:

- tea garden owners
- estate managers
- garden office staff
- payroll staff
- factory staff
- store staff
- senior staff checking daily reports

Assume:

- they are busy
- they know tea garden operations
- they may not know software terms
- they may not trust public claims without proof
- they may prefer local support over self-service software

Do not write for:

- startup investors
- generic SaaS buyers
- software engineers
- corporate IT teams first

## Copy Positioning

Use this internal positioning:

`GardenSuite is an offline-first tea garden management system by Sarbani Associates. It connects face attendance, smart weighing, payroll, factory accounts, stores, and daily reports.`

Use this simpler page-level version:

`GardenSuite helps tea gardens manage attendance, leaf weight, payroll, factory, stores, and daily reports from one system. It works offline at the garden and syncs when internet is available.`

## Product Page Job

Each product page must answer 6 questions:

1. What problem does this module solve?
2. What exactly does GardenSuite do here?
3. What tea garden workflow does it fit into?
4. What changes for the buyer after using it?
5. Will Sarbani Associates help set it up?
6. What is the next step?

If a page does not answer these, the copy is incomplete.

## Recommended Page Formula

Use this structure for every product page:

1. Hero
2. Old way problem
3. GardenSuite workflow steps
4. Result or metric strip
5. On-site rollout section
6. Role-specific buyer benefit
7. Confidentiality note
8. CTA

This is already close to the current structure. Keep it consistent.

## Hero Copy Rules

Hero headline should:

- be short
- name the outcome
- avoid technical terms
- avoid exaggerated absolutes

Good:

- `Every worker verified. Every leaf weighed.`
- `Payroll from real attendance and leaf weight.`
- `Know what every kg of tea costs to make.`
- `Daily garden reports on your phone.`
- `Every store item tracked from purchase to issue.`

Avoid:

- `Transform tea garden operations`
- `Unlock seamless workforce intelligence`
- `Zero leakage forever`
- `The most advanced ERP for estates`

Hero paragraph should explain the mechanism:

Good:

`Face attendance checks worker identity. The wireless scale records leaf weight against the same worker. Both work offline and sync when internet returns.`

Bad:

`Our advanced biometric platform empowers real-time operational efficiency.`

## Problem Copy Rules

Problem cards should name pain that garden staff already recognize.

Use:

- `Registers arrive late`
- `Proxy attendance`
- `Fake weight chits`
- `Payroll takes days`
- `Stock is out of date`
- `Factory costs stay hidden`
- `Daily reports arrive late`

Avoid:

- vague pain like `manual work is hard`
- dramatic fear without proof
- shaming staff
- claims that sound like accusation

Good:

`Workers may mark attendance for others. You may pay for people who did not show up.`

Careful:

`Workers mark friends as present. You pay for people who never showed up.`

The careful version is stronger, but can sound accusatory. Use softer wording when the page needs trust.

## Solution Copy Rules

Always connect feature to workflow.

Use this pattern:

`Feature -> what it records -> where the data goes -> what work becomes easier`

Example:

`The wireless scale sends leaf weight to the worker record. Payroll and daily reports use the same data.`

Avoid:

`Smart weighing improves productivity.`

That is too broad.

## Jargon Rules

Some terms are useful, but pair them with plain meaning.

Use:

- `Daily Report (MIS Dashboard)`
- `ERP (office software)`
- `online dashboard`
- `syncs when internet returns`
- `PF and ESI reports`

Avoid alone:

- `MIS`
- `ERP`
- `cloud`
- `statutory compliance`
- `reconciliation`
- `real-time`

Good:

`The daily report (MIS dashboard) opens on phone, tablet, or laptop.`

Bad:

`Cloud MIS enables real-time visibility.`

## Absolute Claim Rules

Avoid claims that are too absolute unless the product team can prove them in sales and support.

Avoid:

- `100% automated`
- `Zero paper`
- `Stops proxy attendance completely`
- `No errors`
- `Never lose track`
- `Guaranteed`

Prefer:

- `Reduces paper work`
- `Helps stop proxy attendance`
- `No repeated entry from paper registers`
- `Fewer manual mistakes`
- `Keeps a clear record`
- `Designed to reduce leakage`

Current items to soften over time:

- `Zero paper`
- `100% Automated`
- `Zero manual data entry mistakes`
- `Never lose track of a single item`

These can stay if they are clearly demo/sample language, but they are safer when softened.

## Confidentiality Proof Rules

Do not force client names if estates prefer privacy.

Use this standard wording:

`Many estates keep software details private. We respect confidentiality and share region-level experience.`

Use region-level proof:

- Assam
- Dooars
- Terai
- Darjeeling
- Coochbehar
- Uttar Dinajpur
- Jalpaiguri

Use anonymous proof when needed:

- `Dooars estate`
- `Terai estate`
- `Assam estate`
- `North Bengal estate`

Do not invent exact worker counts, estate names, or results unless verified.

## Sarbani Associates Trust Rules

Mention Sarbani Associates in trust-building areas, not only footer.

Use:

- `Built and supported by Sarbani Associates`
- `Sarbani team visits your garden`
- `On-site setup and staff training`
- `Bagdogra, Siliguri`
- `Serving tea gardens since 2000`

Avoid:

- making GardenSuite sound like a faceless SaaS company
- hiding local support until the footer
- overloading every section with company history

## Role-Specific Copy Rules

Role lines are good. Keep them short and practical.

Good:

- `Manager: Know exactly who showed up today`
- `Office staff: Finish payroll in minutes, not days`
- `Owner: Check daily numbers from your phone`
- `Manager: Know stock levels without calling the store`
- `Owner: See per-kg cost without asking anyone`

Avoid:

- `Owner: Unlock operational command`
- `Manager: Empower workforce optimization`
- `Office staff: Achieve seamless processing`

## CTA Copy Rules

CTA should be plain and low-pressure.

Good:

- `Book Free Demo`
- `Email Us`
- `See GardenSuite at your garden`
- `See this module with your data`

Avoid:

- `Start transforming today`
- `Unlock growth now`
- `Get instant access`
- `Request enterprise consultation`

Add confidence below CTA:

`Reply within 1 working day. Demo scheduling call by the Sarbani team.`

This is good because it sets expectation without pressure.

## Dos

- Do write in short sentences.
- Do name the tea garden workflow.
- Do use GardenSuite and Sarbani Associates together in trust areas.
- Do explain what happens to the data.
- Do show how offline work continues.
- Do use region-level proof.
- Do respect client confidentiality.
- Do use buyer-role lines.
- Do pair technical words with plain words.
- Do keep CTAs direct and calm.

## Don'ts

- Do not use em dashes.
- Do not use generic SaaS copy.
- Do not overpromise with absolutes.
- Do not write long sentences with many clauses.
- Do not use English that sounds impressive but is hard to understand.
- Do not expose client names without permission.
- Do not invent numbers.
- Do not say `cloud`, `ERP`, or `MIS` without explanation.
- Do not make the buyer feel blamed.
- Do not make the product sound self-serve when setup is on site.

## Current Product Page Audit

### What Is Working

- The product pages are specific to tea gardens.
- Each page has a clear old-way problem section.
- The workflow steps make the system easier to understand.
- Rollout sections reduce buyer fear.
- Role-specific CTA lines are practical.
- Confidentiality copy solves the client-name problem honestly.
- Sarbani Associates appears in setup and trust context.

### What To Improve Next

- Replace remaining `Zero`, `100%`, and `completely` style claims with softer operational wording.
- Pair `ERP`, `MIS`, and `cloud` with plain-English labels every time.
- Reduce repeated `real-time` language unless the module truly updates live.
- Make meta descriptions simpler and more search-friendly.
- Keep product pages visually consistent in CTA and rollout layout.
- Add stronger module screenshots where available, especially Factory and Stores.

## Final Copy Checklist

Before shipping any product-page copy, check:

- Does the headline say a clear outcome?
- Does the paragraph explain how it works?
- Are technical words explained?
- Is the copy understandable to a non-fluent English reader?
- Is the claim defensible in a demo?
- Is Sarbani Associates visible in trust areas?
- Is client confidentiality handled honestly?
- Is the CTA plain?
- Could this line appear on any generic ERP site? If yes, rewrite it.

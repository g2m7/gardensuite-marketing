# GardenSuite Landing Page - Copy Direction

## Copy Goal

GardenSuite copy should sound:

- simple
- specific
- operational
- trustworthy
- local to tea garden work

It should not sound:

- inflated
- startup-generic
- abstract
- overly polished at the cost of clarity

## Core Messaging Priority

The user should understand this order:

1. What GardenSuite is
2. Who it is for
3. What connected workflow it controls
4. Why it is more trustworthy than paper and disconnected software
5. Why Sarbani Associates is credible

## Positioning Sentence

Working internal version:

`GardenSuite is an offline-first tea garden ERP by Sarbani Associates for attendance, smart weighing, payroll, factory accounts, stores, and daily MIS reporting.`

This is not final homepage copy. It is the message anchor.

## Writing Rules

- Use regular hyphens only
- Use simple English
- Prefer short sentences
- Use real nouns before benefits
- Tie claims to mechanisms
- Mention Sarbani Associates in trust-building sections and footer

## Good Direction

- `Face attendance verifies the worker before hazira is marked.`
- `The wireless scale sends leaf weight directly to the linked worker record.`
- `Payroll is calculated from attendance, rates, deductions, PF, ESI, and bonus rules.`
- `Owners can check the daily MIS from outside the garden.`

## Bad Direction

- `Transform your workforce operations`
- `Unlock seamless productivity`
- `One smart platform for growth`
- `AI-powered efficiency for your business`

## Why The Current Copy Feels Weak

- too many broad slogans
- not enough category framing
- not enough tea-garden language early
- too many claims that are not immediately proven visually
- too many sections say the same thing in different wording

## Section-by-Section Writing Direction

### Hero

Job:

- define the category fast
- show the operational scope
- keep the emotional weight restrained

Target:

- one strong headline
- one clarifying paragraph
- trust line close by

### Proof Strip

Job:

- remove doubt fast

Use:

- estate names
- region coverage
- Sarbani Associates support line

### Connected Workflow

Job:

- explain the chain from worker verification to daily report

Use:

- step-based writing
- specific transitions between modules

Avoid:

- broad “everything connected” language without naming the chain

### Module Sections

Job:

- explain what each module does
- show where it fits in the day

Pattern:

- clear headline
- one paragraph
- two or three factual supporting points at most

### Trust Section

Job:

- explain why the system fits tea gardens specifically

Use:

- tea-garden logic built in
- offline continuity
- on-site setup and training
- long operating history

### CTA

Job:

- ask for the next step plainly

Use:

- `Book a Free Demo`
- `See How GardenSuite Works at Your Garden`

Avoid:

- dramatic closing language
- startup-style urgency phrasing

## Vocabulary To Prefer

- tea garden
- estate
- hazira
- attendance
- plucking
- leaf weight
- payroll
- factory
- stores
- daily MIS
- owner
- manager
- supervisor
- offline
- on-site setup

## Vocabulary To Avoid

- ecosystem
- transformation
- next-gen
- frictionless
- empower
- optimize everything
- end-to-end visibility without context

## Copy Quality Test

If a sentence could be placed on a random HR, logistics, ERP, or field-force software homepage without changing a word, it is probably too generic for GardenSuite.
